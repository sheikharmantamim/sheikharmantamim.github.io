Biography

Hello! I am Sheikh Arman Tamim[cite: 1], an incoming PhD student in the Department of Economics at Texas A&M University. My primary research interests lie in Development Economics [cite: 55] and Labor Economics[cite: 56], with a strong focus on understanding poverty dynamics in developing countries and evaluating the long-term impacts of anti-poverty programs.

Prior to joining Texas A&M, I worked as a Senior Research Associate at the BRAC Institute of Governance and Development (BIGD) [cite: 9, 10], where I managed survey designs and evaluated the trajectory of Ultra Poor Graduate Program (UPGP) participants[cite: 14, 15]. I hold an MA in Quantitative Economics from The University of Alabama [cite: 3] and a BSS in Economics from the University of Dhaka[cite: 4, 5].

Beyond my academic work and coding in Stata and LaTeX, I enjoy playing table tennis in my free time.
