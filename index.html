Biography

Sheikh Arman Tamim
Senior Research Associate
BRAC Institute of Governance and Development

Welcome to my website!

I am an incoming Ph.D. student in Economics at Texas A&M University, starting in the fall session. I currently serve as a Senior Research Associate at the BRAC Institute of Governance and Development (BIGD).

My research primarily focuses on development and labor economics. I am particularly interested in the existence of poverty traps, intra-household resource allocation, and how policy interventions can address key economic challenges in developing countries. Currently, my work includes analyzing the long-term impacts of BRAC's Ultra-Poor Graduation (UPG) program.

Before joining BIGD, I earned my M.A. in Quantitative Economics from The University of Alabama. During my time there, I served as a Teaching Assistant for Advanced Microeconomic Theory, Econometrics, International Trade, and Macroeconomics, taking on responsibilities such as conducting weekly review classes, holding office hours, and grading assignments. I also hold a B.S.S. in Economics from the University of Dhaka.

Research Interests

  • Labor Economics
  • Family Economics
