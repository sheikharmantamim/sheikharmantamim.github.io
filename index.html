Biography

Hello! I am Sheikh Arman Tamim, an economics researcher. My primary research interests lie in Development Economics and Labor Economics, with a strong focus on understanding poverty dynamics in developing countries and evaluating the long-term impacts of anti-poverty programs.

I currently work as a Senior Research Associate at the BRAC Institute of Governance and Development (BIGD), where I manage survey designs and evaluate the trajectory of Ultra Poor Graduate Program (UPGP) participants.

I hold an MA in Quantitative Economics from The University of Alabama and a BSS in Economics from the University of Dhaka. I am highly proficient in quantitative research methodologies and programming in Stata, R, and Python.
